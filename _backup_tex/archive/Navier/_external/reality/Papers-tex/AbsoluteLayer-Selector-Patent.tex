\section*{Field}
This disclosure relates to computer-implemented calibration for instruments and simulators. It provides a method, system, and non-transitory medium for selecting a unique \emph{AbsoluteLayer} by solving simultaneous \emph{dimensionless gate identities} and, from that solution, deriving absolute scales and constants ($\tau_0,\ \ell_0,\ c,\ \hbar,\ \lambda_{\mathrm{rec}},\ G$) \emph{without external anchors}.

\section*{Background}
Calibration frameworks commonly rely on external standards or tuned parameters to set absolute scales. These practices introduce drift and reduce reproducibility across devices and sites. Prior approaches do not provide a \emph{general} machinery to: (i) express calibration constraints as dimensionless \emph{gate identities}; (ii) solve them \emph{simultaneously} to produce a \emph{unique} layer; and (iii) emit absolute scales/constants \emph{anchor-free} with auditable evidence.

\section*{Summary}
Disclosed is an \emph{AbsoluteLayer Selector} that:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
  \item constructs a set of \textbf{dimensionless gate identities} $\mathcal{I}=\{\Phi_j(\mathbf{z})=0\}_{j=1}^m$ over factorized displays;
  \item solves the identities \textbf{simultaneously} under declared tolerance bands and convergence criteria (\emph{MeetsBands});
  \item enforces \textbf{uniqueness} (\emph{UniqueCalibration}): a single admissible solution exists; otherwise the calibration fails closed;
  \item \textbf{derives} $\tau_0,\ \ell_0,\ c,\ \hbar,\ \lambda_{\mathrm{rec}},\ G$ \textbf{anchor-free} from the solution; and
  \item records signed logs summarizing identities, residuals, solver status, solution values, and outputs.
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Definitions}
\noindent\textbf{Gate identities.} A set of dimensionless constraints that must hold simultaneously at the AbsoluteLayer; examples include K-identities and a Planck-gate identity:
\begin{align*}
\text{K-identities:}\quad &\frac{\tau_{\mathrm{rec}}}{\tau_0} = \frac{\lambda_{\mathrm{kin}}}{\ell_0} = K,\\
\text{Planck gate:}\quad &\frac{c^3\,\lambda_{\mathrm{rec}}^2}{\hbar\,G} = C_{\mathrm{layer}}\ (\text{constant by layer}).
\end{align*}

\noindent\textbf{Simultaneous solve.} A numerical or analytic procedure that enforces all identities together (e.g., root finding, constrained optimization) subject to tolerance bands and convergence criteria (\emph{MeetsBands}).

\noindent\textbf{UniqueCalibration.} A uniqueness condition requiring a single admissible solution in the declared band; multiple or no solutions cause fail-closed behavior.

\noindent\textbf{Anchor-free derivation.} Emitting absolute scales $\tau_0,\ \ell_0$ and constants $c,\ \hbar,\ \lambda_{\mathrm{rec}},\ G$ from the identity solution without referencing external standards.

\noindent\textbf{Compliance artifacts.} Signed, hash-addressed logs including identity forms, residuals, solver settings, tolerance bands, solution vectors, and emitted outputs.

\section*{Detailed Description}
\subsection*{Identity Construction}
Gate identities are formed as dimensionless equalities from canonically factorized displays, e.g., K-identities equating independent routes to a common invariant and a Planck-gate identity constraining layer-constant ratios. Policies may declare additional identities to overdetermine the system for robustness.

\subsection*{Simultaneous Solve and Uniqueness}
All identities are enforced together via root finding or constrained optimization. Band constraints (\emph{MeetsBands}) specify numeric tolerances and convergence thresholds. A uniqueness check (\emph{UniqueCalibration}) requires exactly one admissible solution; otherwise the calibration fails closed with diagnostics.

\subsection*{Anchor-Free Derivation}
From the solution, absolute scales $\tau_0,\ \ell_0$ and constants $c,\ \hbar,\ \lambda_{\mathrm{rec}},\ G$ are derived purely from internal identities and policies, without external standards or tuned parameters. Confidence intervals are obtained from band widths and solver sensitivities.

\subsection*{Compliance and Logging}
The selector emits signed, hash-addressed logs including identity definitions, residuals, solver diagnostics, tolerance settings, solution vectors, and emitted outputs. A compliance API exposes these artifacts to downstream consumers and auditors; optional registry publication enables transparency.

\section*{Technical Effects}
The selector yields: (i) anchor-free, reproducible absolute calibration across devices and sites; (ii) deterministic verdicts via uniqueness and band criteria; (iii) robustness by overdetermined identity sets; and (iv) audit-ready evidence for conformance and enforcement.

\section*{Enablement and Best Mode}
Reference implementations include identity libraries (K-identities, Planck gate), solvers with band and uniqueness checks, sensitivity calculators, and signed logging/registry support. Benchmarks report solution rates, residual distributions, and robustness to identity noise.

\section*{Alternative Embodiments}
Identities may be domain-specific and extended. Solvers may employ different numerical methods provided simultaneous enforcement and band constraints are preserved. Outputs may include additional derived quantities as applicable.
