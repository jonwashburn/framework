\section*{Field}
This disclosure relates to real-time scheduling for instruments and multi-actuator control systems. It provides a method, system, and non-transitory medium for scheduling commits in Gray-aligned \emph{eight-tick} windows, enforcing neutrality ($\sum_{i=1}^{8}\delta_i=0$ with mirrored halves), and minimizing a J-kernel block-sum to suppress alias and phase errors in measurements and control.

\section*{Background}
Periodic operations (sensing, integration, actuator updates) are susceptible to aliasing and modal cross-interference when update timing aligns with plant resonances or when residuals accumulate. Equal-spaced or co-phased schedules often reinforce low-order modes and produce drift. There is a need for a scheduler that: (i) uses \emph{eight-tick} minimality (in $D=3$) with Gray alignment; (ii) enforces \emph{neutrality} per block; and (iii) \emph{minimizes a J block-sum} objective to reduce aliasing.

\section*{Summary}
Disclosed is an Eight-Tick Neutral Scheduler that:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
  \item partitions each period into eight windows with Gray-aligned commits and mirrored halves (mid-cycle FLIP);
  \item enforces neutrality per block: $\sum_{i=1}^{8}\delta_i=0$ and $\delta_{i+4}=-\delta_i$ for $i=1,\dots,4$;
  \item minimizes a block-sum of the convex symmetric J-kernel $J(x)=\tfrac12(x+1/x)-1$ over windowed observables to suppress alias and phase errors; and
  \item emits compliance logs with timing, neutrality metrics, J block-sums, and signatures.
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Definitions}
\noindent\textbf{Eight-tick window.} A block of eight sequential windows $(W_1,\dots,W_8)$ that spans the minimal spatially complete period in $D=3$.

\noindent\textbf{Gray-aligned commits.} Window transitions and commits are arranged to change one timing state at a time, minimizing switching artifacts and ensuring deterministic phase behavior.

\noindent\textbf{Neutrality.} For per-window signed updates $\delta_i$, neutrality requires $\sum_{i=1}^{8}\delta_i=0$ and mirrored halves $\delta_{i+4}=-\delta_i$ ($i=1..4$).

\noindent\textbf{J-kernel block-sum.} For a per-window observable or residual sequence $x_i>0$, the scheduler minimizes $\sum_{i=1}^{8} J(x_i)$, where $J(x)=\tfrac12(x+1/x)-1$, to suppress alias/phase errors.

\noindent\textbf{Compliance API.} Interface exposing timing parameters, neutrality observables, J block-sum metrics, and signed logs for audit.

\section*{Detailed Description}
\subsection*{Scheduler Core}
Each period is partitioned into eight windows $(W_1..W_8)$. Gray alignment ensures only one timing state changes at a time. Mirrored halves enforce $\delta_{i+4}=-\delta_i$ with a mid-cycle FLIP. Dwell and slew constraints are enforced to guarantee realizability.

\subsection*{Neutrality Enforcement}
Per-window signed updates $\delta_i$ (e.g., integrated exposure, actuator postings) satisfy $\sum_{i=1}^{8}\delta_i=0$. Neutrality may be verified from synchronized sensing via a block-sum neutrality observable $Z=\sum_{i=1}^{4}(M_i-M_{i+4})$ expected to be zero-mean within declared bands.

\subsection*{J Block-Sum Minimization}
For positive per-window observables $x_i$, a convex symmetric cost $J(x)=\tfrac12(x+1/x)-1$ is minimized over the block to reduce alias/phase errors. Robust aggregation (median/trimmed mean) may be used against outliers. Alias suppression is evidenced by improved metrics relative to equal-spaced baselines.

\subsection*{Compliance and Logging}
The scheduler records timing parameters, jitter, neutrality metrics, J block-sum values, and acceptance verdicts in signed, hash-addressed logs. A compliance API exposes current window state, neutrality flags, and block-sum metrics for audit and downstream gating.

\section*{Technical Effects}
The scheduler yields: (i) reduced alias/phase error via neutrality and J minimization; (ii) deterministic periodic invariance with mirrored halves; (iii) robustness to timing perturbations through Gray alignment and jitter bounds; and (iv) auditability via signed logs and compliance API.

\section*{Enablement and Best Mode}
Reference implementations include an FPGA/SoC timing engine, raised-cosine window envelopes, a streaming J-kernel core with a block-sum accumulator, and synchronized sensing for neutrality verification. Benchmarks quantify alias reduction vs equal-spaced schedules.

\section*{Alternative Embodiments}
Window envelopes may vary (provided $C^1$ smoothness or policy criteria). Jitter budgets, dwell/slew constraints, and aggregation rules can be tuned per modality. Additional windows ($>8$) may be used while preserving mirrored neutrality.
