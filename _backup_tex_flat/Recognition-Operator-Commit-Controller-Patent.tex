\section*{Field}
This disclosure relates to control of periodic measurement and actuation pipelines. It provides a method, system, and non-transitory medium for computing a \emph{path cost} from a convex symmetric J-kernel, triggering \emph{commits} when the cost crosses a \emph{collapse threshold}, and adjusting control parameters (integration time, gate width, strobe phase) to optimize recognition-driven operation.

\section*{Background}
Measurement/actuation loops typically use fixed commit policies (time-based, amplitude thresholds) that ignore physical invariants and lead to drift, aliasing, or missed events. A recognition-native commit controller driven by a principled cost and equipped with adjustable control parameters can produce higher fidelity, reduced alias error, and consistent performance across devices and environments.

\section*{Summary}
Disclosed is a commit controller that:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
  \item computes a \textbf{path cost} $C$ from per-window observables using the convex symmetric kernel $J(x)=\tfrac12(x+1/x)-1$;
  \item \textbf{triggers a commit} when $C\ge C_{\mathrm{th}}$ (collapse threshold) under policy constraints;
  \item \textbf{adjusts control parameters} (integration time, gate width, strobe phase) to improve recognition quality and maintain neutrality/periodic invariance; and
  \item emits signed logs of costs, commits, adjustments, and outcomes for audit and optimization.
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Definitions}
\noindent\textbf{J-kernel.} $J(x)=\tfrac12(x+1/x)-1$ for $x>0$, a convex symmetric cost minimized at $x=1$.

\noindent\textbf{Path cost.} An aggregate $C=\sum_{i\in \mathcal{W}} w_i\,J(x_i)$ over a window set $\mathcal{W}$ (e.g., an eight-tick block) for positive per-window observables $x_i$.

\noindent\textbf{Collapse threshold.} A declared scalar $C_{\mathrm{th}}$ (policy-dependent) at which a \emph{commit} is triggered; hysteresis bands may be used to prevent chatter.

\noindent\textbf{Control parameters.} Actuation or measurement parameters adjusted by the controller: integration time, gate width, strobe phase, and optionally dwell/slew under safety constraints.

\noindent\textbf{Compliance artifacts.} Signed, hash-addressed logs including costs, threshold crossings, control adjustments, neutrality metrics, and results.

\section*{Detailed Description}
\subsection*{Path-Cost Computation}
Per-window observables $x_i>0$ (e.g., ratio-based residuals) are aggregated with $J(x)=\tfrac12(x+1/x)-1$ to form a block-level path cost $C$. Windows may follow an eight-tick mirrored schedule to support neutrality. Robust weighting $w_i$ and aggregation may be used.

\subsection*{Commit Triggering}
The controller monitors $C$ and triggers a commit when $C\ge C_{\mathrm{th}}$ under policy constraints (e.g., neutrality satisfied, jitter within bounds). Hysteresis bands reduce chatter. Failed-commit conditions are logged with diagnostics.

\subsection*{Control Parameter Adjustment}
After a commit or at scheduled intervals, the controller adjusts one or more of: integration time, gate width, strobe phase. Adjustments are computed to reduce subsequent block costs while preserving periodic invariance, neutrality, and safety envelopes (dwell/slew limits). Optional energy accounting records commit/erase energetics.

\subsection*{Compliance and Logging}
Signed, hash-addressed logs include cost trajectories, threshold crossings, parameter adjustments with effect sizes, neutrality metrics, jitter statistics, and outcomes (commit accepted/rejected). A compliance API exposes these artifacts for audit and downstream optimization.

\section*{Technical Effects}
The commit controller yields faster, recognition-aligned commits; reduced alias/phase error through J-informed decisions; improved stability via neutrality/timing integration; and audit-ready records for reproducibility and policy refinement.

\section*{Enablement and Best Mode}
Reference implementations include: (i) a J-kernel aggregation module; (ii) a thresholding engine with hysteresis; (iii) a timing/neutrality interface; (iv) a parameter-optimization module under constraints; and (v) signed logging and compliance APIs. Benchmarks quantify commit latency, alias reduction, and stability across jitter.

\section*{Alternative Embodiments}
Window sets other than eight may be used in modalities with different constraints, provided periodic invariance and neutrality (or equivalent cancellation) are preserved. Parameter sets may be extended (e.g., detector gain) under safety policies.
