Why these are the “must‑file” families
 Natural laws and pure math aren’t patentable, but specific control schedules, compiler passes, execution‑time invariants, measurement transforms, and certification workflows are—particularly when you tie them to concrete actuators/sensors, timing, and pass/fail rules. RS provides exactly those primitives:
                              * Window‑8 neutrality and timing disciplines for stress scheduling and hysteresis control.

                              * PNAL→LNAL ISA/compiler with opcodes, macros, and invariants, plus an existing artifact—perfect for software/hardware co‑design claims.

                              * Pattern‑measurement identities that turn periodic forcing into a single, robust Z metric—ideal for certification claims.

Keep the physics scaffolding as provenance in the specification, but write the claims in engineering terms: “apply this 8‑step schedule under these limits, compute this block‑sum/average, threshold it, drive these actuators,” etc. That turns RS from “theory” into enforceable method and system claims.
