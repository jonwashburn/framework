\noindent\textbf{What the paper proves.}
The manuscript proves a conditional \emph{zero-exclusion} lemma: if an explicit analytic quantity $\mathcal J(s)$
satisfies (i) half--plane positivity $\Re\mathcal J(s)\ge 0$ on $\Omega\setminus Z(\zeta)$, and (ii) blow-up
$\mathcal J(s)\to\infty$ at every $\rho\in Z(\zeta)\cap\Omega$, then $Z(\zeta)\cap\Omega=\varnothing$.
The ``pinch'' step is classical (Cayley transform $\to$ Schur bound $\to$ removability $\to$ maximum modulus);
the nontrivial content is the \emph{formulation and verification} of the hypotheses for this particular $\mathcal J$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Why the assumptions matter.}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.2em]
\item The positivity assumption is exactly what turns $\mathcal J$ into a disk-valued analytic function via a Cayley transform.
Without it, no Schur bound is available, and the pinch contradiction cannot be forced.
\item The blow-up assumption guarantees that the Cayley transform approaches a boundary point ($|\Xi|\to 1$) at a putative zero,
so the singularity becomes removable with boundary value of modulus $1$, triggering the maximum modulus principle.
\end{itemize}

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{What must be added for publishability as a math note.}
A referee will expect at least one \emph{checkable} contribution beyond the classical pinch template:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.4em]
\item \textbf{Verification targets.} Add an explicit ``verification program'' section: identify a concrete region
(e.g.\ $\Re s\ge \sigma_0>1/2$) where $\Re\mathcal J(s)\ge 0$ can be reduced to a bound on $\arg\zeta(s)$ plus
uniformly bounded Euler/determinant remainder terms.
\item \textbf{Quantitative lemmas.} Include uniform bounds for the Euler remainder $\sum_p r_p(s)$ and for the prefactor $(s-1)/s$,
and state a sufficient condition of the form
\[
|\arg \zeta(s)^{-1}|\le \frac{\pi}{2}-\beta(\sigma_0)\quad \Rightarrow\quad \Re\mathcal J(s)\ge 0.
\]
\item \textbf{Prior-work comparison.} Add a short ``Relation to prior criteria'' subsection positioning the work among existing RH
criteria/reformulations, and explain precisely what is new (the specific $\mathcal J$ and its determinant packaging).
\end{enumerate}

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{When it is likely publishable.}
The manuscript is most plausibly publishable as a \emph{sufficient condition / reduction} paper if it contains at least one of:
(i) a nontrivial complex region where positivity is proved; (ii) a stability lemma that makes positivity robust under explicit error terms;
or (iii) a computational/analytic verification on a meaningful domain (with clear reproducibility), while keeping claims strictly conditional.
