## Voice Audit (Recognition-book.tex) — canonical register

Assumption: **Recognition Science is true and publicly accepted**. The goal voice is **canonical**: encountered, not marketed; authoritative, not preachy.

### Voice constitution (distilled from the strongest pages)

The manuscript is at its best (notably in `Prologue`, `In The Beginning`, and `The Ledger`) when it follows these rules:

- **Declare, don’t persuade**: state structure as encountered fact; do not argue with an imagined skeptic.
- **Lead with the real**: concrete scene → name the law; avoid abstract framing before the reader has an anchor.
- **One concept per breath**: introduce one new term, define it plainly, then move; avoid stacking jargon.
- **Keep metaphors mechanical**: metaphors are instruments, not ornaments; avoid repeated “this isn’t a metaphor” throat-clearing.
- **No audience segmentation**: avoid “for skeptics,” “if you feel X, fine,” or belief-management.
- **No author-process / workshop voice**: avoid “to keep this from…,” “this book tries…,” or other meta narration that pulls the reader out of encounter.
- **Timeless register**: avoid dates, “circa 2024,” edition talk, and pop-culture anchors that timestamp the artifact.
- **Rigor without dunking**: avoid snark/gotchas (“bloodletting… trepanning,” “debate about vibes”); keep sober precision.
- **Boundaries belong once**: safety/limits/disclaimers should live in one canonical “boundary box,” not recur as defensive interruptions.
